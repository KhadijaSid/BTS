History

Pre-debut and Training:

Before their debut, members of BTS went through rigorous training under Big Hit Entertainment. Some members, like RM, were underground rappers, while others were aspiring singers and dancers. They underwent training in singing, dancing, rapping, and even language skills to prepare for their debut.

Debut and Early Years:

BTS debuted in 2013 with "2 Cool 4 Skool" and initially gained attention for their socially conscious lyrics, energetic performances, and unique hip-hop sound. In their early years, they faced challenges such as skepticism from the industry and a lack of recognition. However, they gradually built a dedicated fan base through their music and online presence.

Rise to Global Prominence:

BTS's breakthrough came with their "The Most Beautiful Moment in Life" trilogy, which includes albums like "HYYH pt. 1" and "HYYH pt. 2." These albums explored themes of youth, societal pressures, and mental health, resonating with a global audience. Their single "I Need U" from "HYYH pt. 1" marked their first win on a major music show.

International Success and Recognition:

BTS's popularity soared internationally, particularly after their participation in the Billboard Music Awards in 2017, where they won the Top Social Artist award for the first time. Their subsequent releases, such as "Love Yourself: Her" and "Love Yourself: Tear," topped charts worldwide, including the Billboard 200.

Breaking Barriers and Achieving Milestones:

BTS shattered numerous records and barriers in the music industry. They became the first K-pop group to top the Billboard Hot 100 chart with their single "DNA" in 2017 and achieved multiple top-10 hits thereafter. Their album "Love Yourself: Tear" also became the first-ever K-pop album to win the Top Selling Album award at the Gaon Chart Music Awards.

Social Impact and Philanthropy:

BTS consistently uses their platform to address social issues, promote self-love, and spread messages of positivity and acceptance. They've partnered with UNICEF for the "Love Myself" campaign, aimed at ending violence against children and teens globally. BTS has also spoken at the United Nations General Assembly, advocating for youth empowerment and education.

Continuous Growth and Artistic Evolution:

Throughout their career, BTS has continually evolved musically and artistically, experimenting with various genres and themes. They've released albums in multiple languages, collaborated with renowned artists, and ventured into producing their music. BTS's commitment to artistic integrity and authenticity has endeared them to fans worldwide.
